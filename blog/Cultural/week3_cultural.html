Pairing and Feedback

April 19, 2014

Pairing

What was it like to pair with others to solve challenges?

I thought it was interesting pairing to solve challenges, and usually more difficult than I would have thought. I actually thought all my paired challenges went really well and I had good chemistry with my pairs each time, but I just figuring out roles and being on the same page was more challenging than I thought it would be.

Was something particularly fun or rewarding?

Not necessarily fun, but I was interested to see what other people were very good at or had a strong knowledge of relative to me, and conversely what they didn't have as good of a grasp of as I did. For example, I felt comfortable with creating and manipulating objects in JavaScript, while other people seemed to have a better grasp of CSS and creating functions.

Was something frustrating and difficult for you?

Honestly, no, there wasn't anything really frustrating. Other than issues with Google Hangouts or Stypi or bad Internet connections, I enjoyed all my pairing sessions.

Feedback

How did you feel when you read your feedback?

Was the feedback you received helpful to your learning?

Based on the feedback you've received, what are you going to do to improve next time you pair?

Apparently none of my feedback has been rated by other students yet, so I can't really answer these questions.

Added the following on May 27, 2014: So far I think the feedback I've received has been helpful. Mostly it has centered on two things I already knew I needed to work on (emphasis on pseudocoding and just slowing down and not thinking too far ahead), so it's good that the feedback is reinforcing what I already thought were my weaknesses.

How was it to write feedback?

Writing feedback, like pairing, was more difficult than I thought. After a good session I had to wrack my brain to find critiques or things my pair could improve on, and at the same time I didn't want to sound too harsh in articulating those things. I was trying to be specific but it is difficult to be specific without giving away who I am or which session I'm talking about.

What was most difficult for you when writing feedback for your pair?

Again, the hardest thing was articulating my thoughts without sounding unkind or wording it in an unspecific way that isn't particularly helpful to anyone. Also, for example if I suggested something, trying to describe how much of an issue I thought the behavior was (a big issue or a minor issue) was pretty difficult to articulate.

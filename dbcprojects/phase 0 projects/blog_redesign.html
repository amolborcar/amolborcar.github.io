Redesigning My Website

June 1, 2014

Oh, hello there. I've spent a good part of the last week redesigning my website. I know this will be a long process and I expect to continually be adding things to it (not just content but cool design features!) but I hope it's in a good state right now.

What struck me is that I had no idea how I actually wanted my site to look. I guess I don't really consider WHAT makes a website user-friendly or makes the UX enjoyable; rather, I know when I like a website and when I don't like a website. Translating that to my personal site was more challenging though. I decided to search through a lot of professional pages and see how they approached things (without any outright stealing, of course). Most of the better sites I saw were for people advertising their web design skills (surprise surprise) and I know I am not very good at that stuff, so I decided to go for simplicity and readability.

I made a lot of the site using Twitter Bootstrap which made the process much easier. I also played around a bit with some graphical features in jQuery, some of which made it into the site (for example on my blog welcome page) and others that are still a work in progress. Until now I hadn't really given too much thought to my site, unfortunately, but now I do feel like I want it to look nice and have people be impressed when they visit. I have not yet optimized it for different browser sizes or anything like that

Anyway, if you the reader has any suggestions (or finds any bugs, which there are probably still some) please let me know!
